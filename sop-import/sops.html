Best Practices

20 practices
◆Write for one persona per piece
High
Pick a single ICP before writing. Content aimed at everyone converts no one. Define the reader — e-commerce seller, marketplace seller, or digital marketer — and write directly to them.
Source: Best Practices for Blogs
◆Aim for 1,500–2,500 words for SEO blogs
High
In-depth guides and pillar content should target 1,500–2,500 words. Some data suggests 2,450+ words for significantly higher search traffic. Quality over quantity — never add filler to hit a word count.
Source: Best Practices for Blogs
◆Publish 2–3 times per week for traffic growth
High
For traffic and SEO goals, 2–3 posts per week is the recommended cadence. Smaller teams should aim for 1–2 high-quality posts weekly. Consistency matters more than volume.
Source: Best Practices for Blogs
◆Match content to search intent before writing
High
Before writing, determine if the intent is informational, navigational, transactional, or commercial investigation. Tailor the content format and depth to match that intent exactly.
Source: Best Practices for Blogs
◆Repurpose every long-form piece into 3 formats
High
Blog → LinkedIn post → email snippet. Maximises reach per hour of work. Always repurpose before starting new content. Promote via social, email newsletters, and community channels.
Source: Best Practices for Blogs
◆Optimise all images for SEO and speed
Medium
Use descriptive keyword-rich filenames (not IMG_001.jpg). Write alt text for every image. Compress using TinyPNG or Smush. Use WebP or JPEG format. Place images near relevant text.
Source: Best Practices for Blogs
◆Always include author bios on every blog post
High
Google EEAT requires transparency. Every blog must have the author's name, credentials, and short bio — especially critical for YMYL topics. Create a separate editorial standards page on the website.
Source: EEAT Best Practices
◆Cite credible sources with anchor-text hyperlinks
High
Link to research papers, publications, and official sources. If statistics or marketplace policies are mentioned, always hyperlink the source with relevant anchor text — never leave a stat uncited.
Source: EEAT Best Practices + Guest Blog Guidelines
◆Build topical authority around a defined niche
High
Establish core topics to build authority about. Create quality content consistently, link to official sites, and monitor off-page reputation. Handle any brand reputation issues professionally and promptly.
Source: EEAT Best Practices
◆Use Semrush tools to monitor content quality
Medium
Semrush Authority Score: checks overall website content quality. Topic Research: finds niche content gaps. SEO Writing Assistant: assesses quality, readability, and SEO score of any draft before publishing.
Source: EEAT Best Practices
◆Use Claude for keyword research with intent mapping
Medium
Prompt: "Give me 30 keywords for [industry] covering awareness, consideration, and decision stages. For each: keyword, search intent, best content format, and difficulty. Group by intent type." Then verify volumes in Semrush.
Source: EEAT Best Practices — Claude Prompts
◆One landing page = one offer, always
High
Dedicate each landing page to a single offer or buyer problem. Multiple goals on one page dilute focus and hurt conversions. Personalise content based on customer behaviour and intent.
Source: Best Practices for Landing Pages
◆Place CTA above the fold on every page
High
At least one CTA must be visible without scrolling. Use contrasting colors, bold fonts, and action language: "Get Started", "Claim Your Offer Now", "Request a Demo". Repeat on longer pages — all leading to the same goal.
Source: Best Practices for Landing Pages
◆Add social proof and trust signals everywhere
High
Include customer testimonials, reviews, ratings, case studies, social mentions. Display SSL certificate, security badges, guarantees, and contact info. These directly increase conversion rates on landing pages and website pages.
Source: Best Practices for Landing Pages
◆Keep lead forms to essential fields only
High
Every extra field decreases conversion rates. Request only what is absolutely needed. Consider multi-step forms to reduce friction. Use clear labels and helpful error messages.
Source: Best Practices for Landing Pages + Website Pages
◆Design mobile-first on every page
High
Mobile users are the majority. All pages must be fully responsive and load fast. Prioritise mobile before desktop. Test regularly with Google PageSpeed Insights. Essential for Google's mobile-first indexing.
Source: Best Practices for Website Pages
◆Implement technical SEO on all pages
High
All pages must have: clean keyword-rich URLs, unique meta titles and descriptions, logical H1/H2/H3 structure, XML sitemap inclusion, Schema markup, and HTTPS SSL. These are non-negotiable for search rankings.
Source: Best Practices for Website Pages
◆A/B test headlines, CTAs, and visuals regularly
Medium
Regularly test page elements — headlines, CTAs, imagery, copy, form layouts. Use heatmapping to understand user behaviour. Continuous improvement is the foundation of CRO.
Source: Website Pages + Landing Pages
◆Maintain consistent branding and tone across all content
Medium
Use consistent colors, fonts, logos, and imagery across all pages. Language must align with SPCTEK brand tone: informative, engaging, direct, plain language. Any mention of SPCTEK must be formatted exactly as written.
Source: Website Pages + Guest Blog Guidelines
◆Follow SPCTEK writing style rules on all content
High
Always: second person (you), active voice, Oxford comma, 'e-commerce', title case headings. Never: "Click here", ampersands (&) unless brand name, obvious sales pitch. Paragraphs: max 150 words, new paragraph every 60–80 words.
Source: SPCTEK Guest Blog Guidelines

Best Practices

10 practices
◆Hook in the first line of every caption
High
The first line is what stops the scroll. Lead with a bold statement, question, or provocative insight. One message per post. End with a clear CTA. Never bury the hook in the middle of the caption.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Caption Rules
◆Use platform-specific hashtag counts
High
Instagram: 3–5 hashtags. Facebook: 1–3. LinkedIn: 3–5. TikTok: 3–5. Twitter/X: 1–2. Never use the same hashtag set across all platforms. Research relevance per post.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Hashtag Rules
◆Design all visuals mobile-first with strong first frame
High
Mobile-first design is mandatory. Clean layout, high readability, strong first frame that hooks within 1–2 seconds. For video: the first frame must grab attention immediately before the viewer scrolls past.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Visual Content Rules
◆Adapt content strategy to B2B vs B2C per platform
High
B2B (LinkedIn, Twitter/X): focus on authority, trust, and leads. Use case studies, insights, problem-solving content. B2C (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook): focus on engagement, emotion, and conversions. Use reels, product showcases, and promotions.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — B2B vs B2C Strategy
◆Target minimum +20% growth every month
High
Every month must show a minimum of +20% improvement across all KPIs: reach, engagement, clicks, and conversions. Review the previous month's data to identify winning content and replicate successful formats.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Growth Rule
◆Max 2 revision rounds on client work
High
Client feedback must be consolidated. Maximum 2 revision rounds per deliverable. Turnaround: 24–48 hours per round. If no client response after 3 follow-ups (24hr, 48hr, 72hr): proceed with best approved version, start scheduling, and inform the client.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Feedback System
◆TikTok: hook-first content always
High
TikTok content must lead with the hook in the first 1–3 seconds. Viewers decide immediately whether to stay or scroll. The hook defines the performance of the entire video — never bury it.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Platform Guidelines
◆Static posts: clean visuals, benefits-focused messaging
Medium
Static product posts must use clean visuals with minimal text. Messaging must focus on benefits, not just features. Promotional posts require strong offer visibility and urgency-driven creatives.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Content Types
◆Always access client accounts via VPN or VM
High
No exceptions. All social media account access must be via Secure VPN or Authorized Virtual Machine only. No personal devices without VPN. No shared passwords outside secure environments. This protects client data and prevents account flags.
Source: SPCTEK Social Media SOP — Security Policy
◆Instagram: visual + reels-first approach
High

5 SOPs
Keyword selection process
5 steps · SEO Team
1
Define target audience & goals: Clearly identify who the blog or landing page targets. Define specific goals — increasing traffic, generating leads, or boosting sales.
2
Keyword research: Use Ubersuggest or Semrush to find relevant keywords. Focus on short-tail, long-tail, similar terms, intent, prompts, and "People Also Ask". Prioritise keywords with KD under 40.
3
Keyword mapping: Assign primary, secondary, and tertiary keywords per piece. Aim for 1–2% keyword density for primary keyword (once or twice per 100 words). Avoid keyword stuffing.
4
Keyword optimisation: Place primary keyword in title, meta description, H1, H2, H3, and image alt text. Distribute secondary and tertiary keywords throughout the content. Create internal links using keyword-rich anchor text.
5
Performance tracking: Monitor keyword rankings using Semrush rank tracking. Analyse performance regularly and make data-driven adjustments to keyword strategy.

Website audit process
5 steps · SEO Team · Monthly
1
Project setup: Create a new project in Semrush for the target website. Define project goals, scope, target keywords, competitors, and industry benchmarks.
2
Data collection: Use Semrush to gather: organic search keywords, backlink profile, technical SEO issues, full site audit results, and competitor analysis.
3
Data analysis: Prioritise issues by impact. Errors = address immediately. Warnings and notices = secondary priority. Aim for a Semrush site health score near 90.
4
Recommendations: Develop actionable fixes for identified issues. Prioritise by potential impact and feasibility. Create a detailed implementation plan. Log in the Audit Report and Recommendations Report (Google Sheets/Doc links in file).
5
Issue resolution: Implement immediate fixes for critical issues affecting functionality, security, or UX. Reduce page load time and improve performance. Run quick check-ups monthly.

Best Practices

12 practices
◆Always prioritise keywords with KD under 40
High
Focus keyword selection on terms with a Keyword Difficulty score under 40. These offer the best balance between search volume and competition — especially for newer or growing websites. Use Semrush or Ubersuggest to verify.
Source: SOP for SEO — Keyword Selection
◆Keep primary keyword density at 1–2%
High
Use your primary keyword once or twice per 100 words — no more. Keyword stuffing triggers Google penalties and hurts readability. Distribute secondary and tertiary keywords naturally throughout the rest of the content.
Source: SOP for SEO — Keyword Mapping
◆Blog posts: target long-tail, question-based keywords
High
For blogs, focus on long-tail keywords with lower competition but higher conversion potential. Incorporate question-based keywords to attract informational queries. Optimise for featured snippets by answering questions directly and concisely in the content.
Source: SOP for SEO — Blog Post Considerations
◆Landing pages: use primary keyword in H1, body, and FAQs
High
For landing pages, prioritise high-conversion keywords aligned with the page goal. Place the primary keyword in the H1, throughout body content, and within FAQs. Optimise URL structure with the keyword. Test different keyword variations to improve performance.
Source: SOP for SEO — Landing Page Considerations
◆Address errors first, warnings second in audits
High
In every website audit, errors are Priority 1 — fix immediately. Warnings and notices are Priority 2. Never deprioritise errors in favour of easier fixes. Aim for a Semrush site health score near 90 as the ongoing target.
Source: SOP for SEO — Website Audit
◆Run website quick check-ups monthly
High
Monthly audits catch regressions before they become serious issues. Include: page speed check (PageSpeed Insights), broken link scan (deadlinkchecker.com), Semrush site audit, and GSC crawl error review every month without exception.
Source: SOP for SEO — Audit Frequency
◆Confirm all 5 tracking codes on every new website
High
Before any SEO work begins on a new website, verify presence of all 5 tracking codes via builtwith.com: GA4 ID, Facebook Meta Pixel, Google Ads Conversion, Google Tag Manager, and Google Search Console. Missing codes = missing data.
Source: SOP for SEO — Initial Audit Marketing Analytics
◆Cover all 3 keyword intent types per content plan
Medium
Every content plan must include a mix of: Informational (how to, what is, best), Navigational (brand/website specific), and Transactional (buy, order, sign up) keywords. This covers the full buyer journey and maximises organic reach across all funnel stages.
Source: SOP for SEO — Keyword Types
◆Analyse competitors before building any content strategy
High
Before starting any new content strategy: identify primary and secondary competitors, analyse their top-ranking keywords, evaluate their on-page SEO, audit their backlink profiles, assess site speed and mobile-friendliness, and review their content quality, length, and frequency.
Source: SOP for SEO — Competitor Analysis
◆Extract and act on GSC data regularly
High
GSC data must be actively used — not just collected. Use Performance data to identify low-CTR high-impression pages (update titles/meta). Use Coverage to fix crawl errors. Use Core Web Vitals to address UX issues. Export as CSV and review at minimum monthly.
Source: SOP for SEO — GSC Data Extraction
◆Embed responsive, captioned videos in relevant blog posts
Medium
Videos increase dwell time and improve SEO signals. Every embedded video must be responsive, have a clear title, and include closed captions for accessibility and SEO. Strategically place the video where it best supports the surrounding content — not arbitrarily.
Source: SOP for SEO — Video in Blog Posts
◆Stay updated via trusted SEO resources weekly
Medium
SEO changes constantly. Follow these sources weekly: Google Search Central Blog, Search Engine Journal, Moz Blog, Ahrefs Blog, Semrush Blog, Search Engine Land, and Backlinko. Algorithm updates can require immediate content or technical adjustments.
Source: SOP for SEO — Resources to Stay Updated
